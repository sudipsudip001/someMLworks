# Regulatory Provisions Governing Voter Eligibility, Participation, and Conduct
## Federal Election of Nepal, 2082 (Voting Period Referenced Herein)


1. Definitions

    1. A Voter shall mean any Nepalese national whose identity appears in the Final Electoral Roll and who has been issued a Voter Identification Card deemed valid for the purposes of this election.

    1. An Eligible Person shall mean a natural person who satisfies all eligibility requirements applicable at the time participation is assessed.

    1. A Participant shall mean any person who is physically present at a designated polling location during any part of the voting period, irrespective of whether a ballot is ultimately cast.

    1. The Voting Period shall mean the duration beginning on Falgun 21, 2082 and concluding upon the official closure of polling activities.

1. Eligibility Requirements

    1. An Eligible Person must have completed eighteen (18) years of age no later than the conclusion of the Voting Period.

    1. For the avoidance of doubt, age shall be determined solely by reference to the year of birth recorded in the national civil registry, expressed in Bikram Sambat (BS).

    1. Persons whose recorded year of birth is 2064 BS or later shall be deemed underage for the purposes of this election.

    1. No person deemed underage under Section 2.3 may be considered an Eligible Person, unless eligibility was established prior to the commencement of the Voting Period.

1. Registration and Territorial Scope

    1. Only Voters whose registration was completed within the territory of Nepal shall be included in the Final Electoral Roll.

    1. Nepalese nationals residing outside the territory of Nepal during the Voting Period shall not participate in the election.

    1. For the purposes of Section 3.2, participation shall include presence, identification verification, or ballot issuance, whether or not a vote is cast.

    1. Notwithstanding Sections 3.1–3.3, a Voter whose registration was completed domestically shall retain voter status until such status is explicitly revoked.

1. . Voting Process and Temporal Constraints

    1. The Voting Period shall consist of two consecutive calendar days, beginning on Falgun 21, 2082.

    1. A Voter may cast one (1) ballot at any time during the Voting Period.

    1. Eligibility to cast a ballot shall be assessed at the time of ballot issuance.

    1. A Participant who was an Eligible Person on the first day of the Voting Period shall not be rendered ineligible solely by a change in circumstances occurring on the second day.

    1. No ballot shall be issued to any person who is ineligible at the time eligibility is assessed under Section 4.3.

1. Integrity Measures and Prohibitions

    1. Upon the casting of a ballot, a permanent mark shall be applied to the left thumb of the Participant, signifying completion of participation.

    1. No person bearing a participation mark shall be permitted to cast a ballot, regardless of voter or eligibility status.

    1. The presence of a participation mark shall constitute conclusive evidence of prior participation.

    1. No Eligible Person shall be denied the right to vote except as expressly prohibited under these provisions.

1. Enforcement and Authority

    1. Election security personnel are authorized to prevent participation by any person reasonably believed to be in violation of any provision herein.

    1. In cases of ambiguity, interpretations that preserve electoral integrity shall prevail over interpretations that expand participation.
