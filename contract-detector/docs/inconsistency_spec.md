# Definition Conflict

**Examples**
- 1.1: "Voter is defined by registration and possession of a voter ID."
- 1.2: "Eligible person is defined by meeting eligibility requirements at the time participation is assessed."
- 1.3. "A Participant is anyone physically present during Voting period, regardless of whether they vote"
- 2.4. "Underage is not eligible, unless eligibility existed earlier."
- 5.4. "States "No eligible person shall be denied the right to vote except as expressly prohibited."

**Description**
The definitions overlap without a clear hierarchy, causing ambiguity about which status governs voting rights.

# Constraint Conflict

**Examples**
- Section 2.1: "Eligible person must be 18 years by the end of Voting period."
- Section 2.3: "The person born in 2064 BS or after are deemed underage."
- Section 2.4: "Underage is not eligible, unless eligibility existed earlier."

**Description**
Mutually exclusive conditions (age, region, role), that seem to contradict each other. The document does not specify how an underage person could ever have eligibility established earlier, making the constraints logically incompatible without extra assumptions.

# Scope Conflict

**Examples**
- Section 3.2: "Nepalese residing outside Nepal during Voting can't participate in election."
- Section 3.3: "Defines participation broadly."
- Section 3.4: "Voter status is retained until explicitly revoked."

**Description**
Rule applies to "all users" vs "some users" and the rule seems to conflict on what really needs to be assumed. The scope of status and permitted actions is misaligned.

# Temporal Conflict

**Examples**
- Section 2.1: "The voter should be 18 by the end of voting."
- Section 4.1: "It's a 2 day voting process."
- Section 4.3: "Eligibility is assessed at the time of ballot issuance."
- Section 4.4: "Someone who's eligible on day 1 remains eligible on day 2."

**Description**
Dates/durations don't align and there's some misalignment in what to make out of the given conditions. This creates ambiguity of whether a person of age 17 on day one, but 18 by day two can vote, on day one, day two or both.

# Permission vs. Prohibition

**Examples**
- 5.2: "Marked persons may not cast a ballot."
- 5.4: "No Eligible person shall be denied the right to vote except as as expressly prohibited."
- 4.2: "One ballot per voter."

**Description**
"Allowed" in one place, "forbidden" in another. The mark itself is evidence of prior participation, not necessarily of ballot casting. The prohibition applies "regardless of voter or eligibility status".

A general permission (right to vote) is overridden by a strict prohibition (presence of a mark), even though:

- The mark itself is evidence of prior participation, not necessarily of ballot casting.
- The prohibition applies “regardless of voter or eligibility status.”
- This pits a protected right against an unconditional ban.
